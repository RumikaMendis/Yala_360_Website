        Wildlife of Yala

        Discover the iconic species that call Yala National Park home.

        Sri Lankan Leopard

        🔴 Endangered

        The Sri Lankan leopard (Panthera pardus kotiya) is the top predator in Yala. With the highest leopard density in the world, Block I is the best zone for sightings, especially during dawn and dusk hours.

        Best ZoneBlock I
        Peak Time5–7 AM
        Population~40 in Yala

        Sri Lankan Elephant

        🟡 Vulnerable

        The Sri Lankan elephant (Elephas maximus maximus) roams in herds across the park's grasslands. Yala hosts over 300 elephants, frequently gathering near waterholes during the dry season.

        Best ZoneBlock II
        Peak Time3–5 PM
        Population300+ in Yala

        Sri Lankan Sloth Bear

        🔴 Endangered

        The sloth bear (Melursus ursinus) is a shy, nocturnal species often spotted foraging among rocky terrain. Block III offers the highest probability of sightings during early mornings.

        Best ZoneBlock III
        Peak Time5–7 AM
        Population~20 in Yala

        Mugger Crocodile

        🟢 Least Concern

        The mugger crocodile (Crocodylus palustris) can be found basking near waterholes and along the Menik Ganga river. These ancient predators are a frequent sight along Block I's waterways.

        Best ZoneBlock I
        Peak TimeAll Day
        Population100+ in Yala
